Welcome to Saturated Fats and Cardiovascular Disease

This is a project to explore data wrangling, unification of data sets, the use of OpenRefine, and data visualization.

Resources

The graph is made using Plotly.Js, a declarative charting library built on top of d3.js and stack.gl.

How this graph was made:

1 Collect data from open sources:

World Health Organization: Mortality, cholesterol.

Oxford University: Department of Public Health, European cardiovascular disease statistics.

2 Clean and unify data sets using OpenRefine.

3 Structure the data.

4 Experiment with different visualization options.

5 Choose the graph that best explains the data. In this case a scatter plot.

Saturated Fat

Is it protective?

Why do people eating the most saturated fat have the least cardiovascular disease?

Could it be because saturated fat is stable at body temperature, it does not oxidize.

So what does oxidize?

Is this why a Mediterranean diet is so good?

Olive oil is mostly monounsaturated fat, which is much more stable at body temperature than vegetable oils. So less oxidation happens.

Is this why the French saturated diet is even better for heart disease?

Saturated fat is more stable than monounsaturated fat. Even less oxidation occurs.

Saturated fat (most stable, has to survive in warm mammals).

Monounsaturated fat (stable, survives in Mediterranean heat).

Polyunsaturated fat (highly unstable, grown in cool climates).

Also, all that monounsaturated or saturated fat helps absorb the fat-soluble vitamins out of foods eaten!
